Key Insights
Analytical findings derived from Hive queries on the full dataset
📈
Q4 Seasonal Surge
Revenue surges dramatically in Q4, with November 2011 reaching £1.46M — nearly 3× the monthly average. This pattern signals strong seasonal demand tied to holiday gifting. Inventory and logistics must be pre-scaled by September.
🌍
UK Market Concentration Risk
The United Kingdom accounts for 83.9% of all revenue (£8.19M). The Netherlands (£284K), EIRE (£263K), and Germany (£221K) are the next largest. Heavy dependence on a single market creates vulnerability to local economic shocks.
🏆
Champions Drive Disproportionate Revenue
Just 961 Champion customers (22% of base) generated £5.61M — over 57% of total revenue. Retention programmes targeting this cohort yield outsized ROI. A single churned Champion costs as much as acquiring ~7 average customers.
⚠️
At-Risk Segment Needs Immediate Action
299 At-Risk customers hold £499K in revenue potential. These customers previously purchased but have gone quiet. Targeted win-back campaigns (discounts, personalised emails) could recover a significant portion before they churn permanently.
⏰
Midday is Prime Sales Time
12:00 noon generates the highest revenue (£1.36M), followed by 10:00 and 15:00. All peak hours fall between 9 AM–5 PM, typical of a B2B wholesale platform. Flash sales and promotions should be scheduled in the 10–13 window for maximum reach.
🔁
Strong Customer Loyalty Foundation
3,059 of 4,372 customers (70%) are repeat buyers. The Dec 2010 cohort retained ~38% of customers through month 2 and settled at ~35–40% by month 12 — a strong retention floor for a wholesale business. Loyalty programmes can push this above 50%.
🛍️
DOTCOM POSTAGE Tops Revenue but Skews Data
DOTCOM POSTAGE earned £206K but is a service charge, not a product. Excluding it makes REGENCY CAKESTAND 3 TIER (£164K) the top physical product. Basket analysis should strip service SKUs to surface true product affinity patterns.
📦
High Average Order Value Indicates Wholesale Behaviour
An AOV of £376 with an average basket of 199.86 units far exceeds typical retail behaviour. This confirms the dataset represents a wholesale/B2B channel where bulk purchasing is standard. Pricing, MOQs and volume discounts should be primary commercial levers.
